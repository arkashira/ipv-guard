# Product Requirements Document (PRD) for ipv-guard
## Overview
The ipv-guard product aims to provide a stable and automated IPv6 prefix management solution for developers and organizations. This document outlines the product requirements, target users, goals, key features, success metrics, and scope for the ipv-guard project.

## Problem Statement
IPv6 prefix management is a complex and error-prone process, particularly for organizations with large and dynamic networks. Manual configuration and management of IPv6 prefixes can lead to errors, security vulnerabilities, and network downtime. The lack of automation and visibility in IPv6 prefix management hinders the ability of organizations to efficiently manage their networks and ensure reliable connectivity.

## Target Users
The primary target users for the ipv-guard product are:

* Network administrators and engineers responsible for managing IPv6 networks
* DevOps teams and developers who require stable and automated IPv6 prefix management for their applications
* Organizations with large and complex networks that require efficient and reliable IPv6 prefix management

## Goals
The goals of the ipv-guard product are to:

* Provide a stable and automated IPv6 prefix management solution for developers and organizations
* Simplify the process of managing IPv6 prefixes and reduce the risk of human error
* Improve network reliability and uptime by ensuring accurate and consistent IPv6 prefix configuration
* Enhance security by implementing robust authentication and authorization mechanisms for IPv6 prefix management

## Key Features (Prioritized)
The following key features are prioritized for the ipv-guard product:

1. **Automated IPv6 Prefix Management**: The ability to automatically manage IPv6 prefixes, including allocation, deallocation, and modification.
2. **IPv6 Prefix Configuration**: A user-friendly interface for configuring IPv6 prefixes, including support for multiple prefix formats and lengths.
3. **Network Discovery and Mapping**: The ability to discover and map IPv6 networks, including automatic detection of network topology and prefix usage.
4. **Real-time Monitoring and Alerts**: Real-time monitoring of IPv6 prefix usage and alerts for potential issues, such as prefix exhaustion or misconfiguration.
5. **Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)**: Implementation of RBAC to ensure that only authorized users can manage IPv6 prefixes and access network configuration.
6. **Integration with Existing Network Management Tools**: Integration with popular network management tools, such as Ansible, Puppet, and Chef.

## Success Metrics
The success of the ipv-guard product will be measured by the following metrics:

* **Customer Satisfaction**: Measured through surveys, feedback forms, and support ticket resolution rates.
* **Network Uptime and Reliability**: Measured through monitoring of network downtime and error rates.
* **Adoption and Retention**: Measured through the number of customers who adopt and continue to use the ipv-guard product.
* **Security and Compliance**: Measured through regular security audits and compliance with industry standards and regulations.

## Scope
The scope of the ipv-guard product includes:

* Development of a web-based user interface for managing IPv6 prefixes
* Implementation of automated IPv6 prefix management and network discovery and mapping
* Integration with existing network management tools
* Development of a robust and scalable architecture to support large and complex networks

## Out-of-Scope
The following features and functionalities are out-of-scope for the initial release of the ipv-guard product:

* Support for IPv4 prefix management
* Integration with cloud-based network management platforms
* Development of a mobile application for ipv-guard

By focusing on the key features and goals outlined in this PRD, the ipv-guard product will provide a stable and automated IPv6 prefix management solution for developers and organizations, simplifying the process of managing IPv6 networks and improving network reliability and security.
