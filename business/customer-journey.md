# Customer Journey Map for ipv-guard

## Aware
- **Trigger Event:** Developers and organizations face issues with IPv6 prefix management and automation, leading to network instability.
- **Friction Points:** Lack of awareness about existing solutions, confusion over IPv6 complexities, and uncertainty about the benefits of automation.
- **User Emotions:** Frustration due to network issues, curiosity about potential solutions, and skepticism about new tools.
- **Opportunities to Delight:** Educational content (blogs, webinars) explaining IPv6 management, showcasing success stories, and clear value propositions.
- **Metric:** Number of website visits and engagement with educational content (e.g., downloads, video views).

## Consider
- **Trigger Event:** Users actively search for solutions to IPv6 management issues.
- **Friction Points:** Overwhelmed by choices, difficulty in comparing features, and concerns about integration with existing systems.
- **User Emotions:** Hopeful for a solution, but anxious about making the wrong choice and the complexity of implementation.
- **Opportunities to Delight:** Interactive comparison tools, detailed product demos, and customer testimonials highlighting ease of use and integration.
- **Metric:** Number of product comparisons made and demo requests.

## Try
- **Trigger Event:** Users decide to test ipv-guard to address their IPv6 management needs.
- **Friction Points:** Initial setup challenges, unclear documentation, and limited trial features.
- **User Emotions:** Excitement about trying a new tool, but potential frustration if the setup is complicated or documentation is lacking.
- **Opportunities to Delight:** Streamlined onboarding process, comprehensive guides, and responsive customer support during the trial phase.
- **Metric:** Trial conversion rate and user feedback on the onboarding experience.

## Adopt
- **Trigger Event:** Users successfully implement ipv-guard and see improvements in their network management.
- **Friction Points:** Learning curve associated with full feature utilization and potential integration issues with existing systems.
- **User Emotions:** Satisfaction from improved stability, but possible concern about ongoing support and updates.
- **Opportunities to Delight:** Regular check-ins, advanced training sessions, and proactive communication about updates and new features.
- **Metric:** User retention rate and feature usage statistics.

## Expand
- **Trigger Event:** Users look for additional features or services to enhance their IPv6 management experience.
- **Friction Points:** Uncertainty about which additional features would be beneficial and potential budget constraints.
- **User Emotions:** Optimism about further improvements, but cautious about costs and complexity.
- **Opportunities to Delight:** Personalized recommendations based on usage patterns, bundled offers for additional features, and loyalty programs.
- **Metric:** Upsell conversion rate and customer satisfaction scores.
